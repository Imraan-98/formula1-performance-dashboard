# 🏎️ Power BI dashboard analysing 70+ years of Formula 1 data, uncovering patterns in driver performance, constructor dominance, and race reliability.

An interactive Business Intelligence dashboard built using Microsoft Power BI to analyse historical Formula 1 data, including driver performance, constructor trends, and race analysis.

---

## 📊 Project Overview

This project explores over 70 years of Formula 1 World Championship data, integrating race results, drivers, constructors, and technical reliability to uncover patterns in competitive success and performance.

---

## 🔍 Key Metrics

- Total Races: 1125  
- Total Drivers: 861  
- Total Wins: 1128  
- Technical Failures: 2480  

These metrics highlight the scale of the dataset and the importance of both performance and reliability in Formula 1.

---

## 📈 Key Insights

- **Global Expansion:**  
  Formula 1 has evolved from a Europe-centric championship to a globally distributed sport, with increasing race presence across North America, Asia, South America, and Australia.

- **Constructor Dominance:**  
  Teams such as Ferrari, McLaren, and Williams demonstrate strong long-term performance and consistency.

- **Performance Trends Over Time:**  
  Constructor points have increased significantly in the modern era, reflecting changes in race formats, points systems, and season length.

- **Reliability Impact:**  
  Technical failures play a critical role in race outcomes, highlighting the importance of engineering reliability alongside driver performance.
